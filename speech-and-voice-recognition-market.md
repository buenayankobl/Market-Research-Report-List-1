<p><p>The speech and voice recognition market encompasses two primary types: speech recognition and voice recognition. Speech recognition focuses on understanding and processing spoken language, converting it into text, and enabling interactions with devices through natural language. In contrast, voice recognition identifies and verifies individual speakers based on their unique vocal characteristics. While both technologies enhance human-computer interaction, speech recognition prioritizes understanding content, whereas voice recognition emphasizes security and user authentication by recognizing distinct voice patterns.</p></p>
