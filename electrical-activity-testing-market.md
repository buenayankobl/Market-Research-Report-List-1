<p><p>The electrical activity testing market encompasses three primary modalities: Electroencephalography (EEG), Magnetoencephalography (MEG), and Electromyography (EMG). EEG measures electrical signals in the brain, aiding in diagnosing neurological disorders. MEG captures the magnetic fields produced by neural activity, offering insights into brain function with high spatial and temporal resolution. EMG assesses electrical activity in muscles, helping to diagnose neuromuscular disorders. Collectively, these technologies provide critical information for clinical and research applications, enhancing understanding of the nervous system.</p></p>
