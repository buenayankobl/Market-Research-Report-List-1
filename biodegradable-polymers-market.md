<p><p>The biodegradable polymers market is divided into two main types: synthetic and natural biodegradable polymers. Synthetic biodegradable polymers are engineered from petroleum-based resources, offering specific properties for applications such as packaging and medical devices. Common examples include polylactic acid (PLA) and polycaprolactone (PCL). Natural biodegradable polymers, derived from renewable biomass sources, include materials like starch, cellulose, and chitosan. These are often used in agriculture, food packaging, and pharmaceuticals, benefiting from lower environmental impact and renewability.</p></p>
