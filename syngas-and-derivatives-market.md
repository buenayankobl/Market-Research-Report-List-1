<p><p>The syngas and derivatives market encompasses various production methods. Partial oxidation involves reacting hydrocarbon feedstocks with oxygen, creating syngas with fewer impurities. Steam reforming uses steam to convert hydrocarbons, producing a hydrogen-rich syngas suitable for fuels and chemicals. Biomass gasification transforms organic materials into syngas through high-temperature processes, promoting renewable energy. Other methods include autothermal reforming and coal gasification, each contributing unique syngas compositions for diverse applications, such as electricity generation and chemical synthesis.</p></p>
