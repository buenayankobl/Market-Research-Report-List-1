<p><p>The artificial cardiac pacemaker market is divided into three main types: traditional pacemakers, implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICDs), and biventricular ICDs. Traditional pacemakers regulate heartbeats in patients with bradycardia. ICDs are designed to detect and correct life-threatening arrhythmias, providing shocks when necessary. Biventricular ICDs offer advanced therapy for heart failure by synchronizing heart contractions through electrical stimulation of both ventricles. Each type plays a crucial role in managing various cardiac conditions, enhancing patient outcomes and quality of life.</p></p>
