<p><p>The dental prostheses market encompasses various types, including acrylic, composite, ceramic, and metal materials. Acrylic prostheses are lightweight and cost-effective, making them popular for temporary solutions. Composite materials offer aesthetic appeal and are used for both crowns and fillings due to their tooth-like appearance. Ceramic prostheses are durable and biocompatible, commonly used for bridges and crowns. Metal prostheses, typically made of alloys, provide strength and longevity, favored for their robustness in restorative applications. Each type serves distinct clinical needs.</p></p>
