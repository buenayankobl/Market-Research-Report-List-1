<p><p>The interlinings fabric market consists primarily of two types: non-woven and woven. Non-woven interlinings are produced by bonding fibers together, resulting in lightweight, flexible materials that offer good drape and breathability, making them ideal for casual garments. Conversely, woven interlinings are crafted by interlacing threads, providing stronger structure and stability, which suits tailored garments. Each type serves distinct purposes in garment construction, influencing the fit, comfort, and overall aesthetic of the finished product.</p></p>
